# Market Potential Report: Indian Takeout Outlet — Chincoteague Island, VA

## Executive Summary

Chincoteague Island has a small, aging permanent population (3,296 residents; median age 57.4) but sits inside a high‑tourism economy where Accomack County reported $192.2M in visitor spending in 2024, including $55.0M on food & beverage. The island itself reportedly welcomed 1.3M visitors in 2024. The opportunity profile is tourism‑exposed and seasonal, but the **quantitative effect of seasonality is not established** in the cited sources. The market sizing below is an **assumption‑based scenario range** of ~$0.26M–$1.30M annually for a single takeout outlet, driven by explicit assumptions on household participation, order frequency, average ticket, and tourist capture. The cited state‑level requirements appear limited to VDH plan review, permit, inspection, and CFPM; local business licensing and any meals/food‑and‑beverage tax require manual verification at town/county level.

**Bottom line:** A takeout‑focused Indian concept is plausible if it targets peak season capture and manages off‑season fixed costs. The economic upside depends heavily on tourism capture and event‑driven spikes; a conservative approach should assume modest local demand and plan for shoulder/low‑season variability.

## 3. Regulatory Requirements (State‑Level, Verified)

### Virginia Department of Health — Food Establishment Permit

- **Plan Review Application:** $40 fee to local health department.
- **Foodservice Permit Application:** $40 fee, required at least 30 days before opening.

## 4. Market Sizing and Financial Model

This model separates **local resident demand** and **tourist demand**. All non‑sourced inputs are labeled assumptions and should be validated with local surveys or POS benchmarks.

### 4.1 Local Resident Demand (Assumption‑Driven)

**Formula:**

```
Local Revenue = Households × Active Share × Orders/Month × 12 × Avg Ticket
```

Inputs (assumptions):

- Households: 1,714 (Census Reporter)
- Active Share: % of households ordering from the outlet
- Orders/Month per active household
- Average ticket size

| Scenario | Active Share | Orders/Month | Avg Ticket | Local Revenue (Annual) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Low | 20% | 2 | $18 | **$148k** |
| Base | 30% | 3 | $22 | **$407k** |
| High | 40% | 4 | $26 | **$856k** |

### 4.2 Tourist Demand (Top‑Down Context Proxy)

**Method:** Use a small share of Accomack County’s 2024 food & beverage spend ($55.0M) as a **context proxy**, not a direct addressable market. The share is a planning assumption for a single outlet’s portion of total county F&B spending.

| Scenario | Capture of County F&B Spend | Tourist Revenue (Annual) |
|---|---:|---:|
| Low | 0.20% | **$110k** |
| Base | 0.40% | **$220k** |
| High | 0.80% | **$440k** |

### 4.3 Total Revenue Range (Local + Tourist)

| Scenario | Local Revenue | Tourist Revenue | Total Revenue |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Low | $148k | $110k | **$258k** |
| Base | $407k | $220k | **$627k** |
| High | $856k | $440k | **$1.296M** |

**Interpretation:** In these scenarios, **local‑demand assumptions drive more revenue than tourist capture** in low/base/high cases. The model is therefore more sensitive to household participation and order frequency than to tourist capture.

## 5. Demand Drivers and Cultural Fit (Evidence‑Based + Cautions)

- **Tourism intensity** is a major context factor, supported by the 1.3M visitor count and county‑wide tourism spending data (WBOC).
- **Older median age** suggests demand for approachable, customizable spice levels and clear menu descriptions.
- **Income levels** are moderate; pricing should remain accessible while offering higher‑margin combos during peak season.

**Data gaps:** No direct local cuisine preference data or restaurant mix data were found in verified sources. A short local survey or analysis of existing restaurant offerings is required to estimate the Indian cuisine adoption rate.

## 6. Key Risks and Unknowns

1. **Seasonality risk:** Peak tourism likely concentrates cash flow into summer months. Quantified monthly visitation data is not available in verified sources.
2. **Local adoption uncertainty:** No verified data on Indian cuisine penetration, restaurant mix, or competitive density.
3. **Regulatory details at town/county level:** Business license fees and local food/beverage taxes need confirmation from official town/county sources.
4. **Labor and supply chain:** Not assessed; rural hiring and specialty ingredient supply may add cost or delay.

---

## 7. Strategic Recommendations (Actionable Next Steps)

1. **Validate seasonality:** Obtain monthly lodging occupancy or visitor counts from local tourism office or town records.
2. **Menu and pricing tests:** Run a 2‑week pop‑up or limited menu test during shoulder season to gauge resident demand.
3. **Confirm local taxes and licenses:** Contact Accomack County Commissioner of Revenue and Chincoteague town offices.
4. **Tourism‑capture planning:** Use 0.4% of county F&B spend as a **planning case**, not a validated target, until island share and takeout share are confirmed.
